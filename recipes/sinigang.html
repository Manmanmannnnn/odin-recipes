Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na baboy is a popular Filipino soup made with pork, vegetables, and a sour broth, usually flavored with tamarind. It's known for its tangy taste and hearty ingredients, making it a comforting dish perfect for any meal.

Ingredients

  • Pork (commonly belly, ribs, or shank)
  • Tamarind (or a sinigang mix for the sour flavor)
  • Water
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Radish
  • Eggplants
  • Stringbeans
  • Fish sauce
  • Green Chilli Pepper
  • Water Spinach

Steps in Cooking

  1. Cook the Pork: In a pot, add the pork and enough water to cover it. Bring to a boil and let it simmer until the pork is tender.
  2. Add Aromatics: Add the sliced onions and quartered tomatoes to the pot. Cook for about 5-10 minutes until softened.
  3. Add Tamarind: Mix in the tamarind (or sinigang mix) and stir well to combine, adjusting the sourness to your taste.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add the radish, eggplant, and string beans. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Finish with Greens: Stir in the water spinach (or other leafy greens) and green chili peppers. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then season with fish sauce before serving hot.
